In this event, we aim to critically explore the intersection of equity and justice in computing. Our motivation stems from the recognition that computing is far from neutral - it intricately carries the values and biases of its creators. Here, we will uncover the layers of harm inflicted by data and algorithms rooted in pre-existing systems of oppression at both individual and structural levels in our society and analyze how these algorithms, seemingly impartial, can exacerbate societal inequalities by amplifying, centralizing, privatizing, automating, and abstracting inequitable social systems. Our overarching objective is to elevate researchers' critical consciousness of computing, equity, and justice. We aim to establish a direct and impactful link between the injustices in their lives and the technology they create. By fostering this connection, we seek to provoke a deep understanding of the ethical dimensions within the realm of computing. Join us on this transformative journey, where we collectively work towards increasing awareness, challenging assumptions, and contributing to a more equitable and just technological landscape.

Keynote Speakers

Kristina Höök
Kristina is a professor in Interaction Design at KTH. Her research interests include affective interaction, somaesthetic design, internet of things and anything that makes life with technology more meaningful, enjoyable, creative and aesthetically appealing.


Teresa Cerratto-Pargman
Teresa is a full professor of Human-machine interaction at at Stockholm University (SU). Her research interests in mainly on developing a critical lens of computing in educational technology.


Anne-Kathrin Peters
Anne-Kathrin is an associate professor of Technology Education with Focus on Sustainability at KTH. Her main interest is the role of education and technology for change towards more just and sustainable ways of living, particularly in the intersection of social sustainability (equality, equity, democracy) and environmental sustainability.
